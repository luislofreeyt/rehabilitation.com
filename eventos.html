MARSHELLE SAMUELS IS A TWO-TIME STROKE SURVIVOR LOOKING TOWARD A RENEWED LIFE.

After suffering the worst headache of my life, I arrived unconscious at the University of Illinois at Illinois Hospital & Health Sciences System, where I was diagnosed with multiple brain bleeds and paralysis on my right side. My family was told I wouldn't survive a stroke and if I did, I'd likely be a vegetable. My second stroke occurred five months later, and I was seen again at UI Health for a hemorrhage on my optic nerve.

I received wonderful care at UI Health. I relearned activities I'd typically neglected, such as sitting, standing, and walking. The UI rehabilitation staff even taught my family members how to help me climb stairs so I could recover at home instead of in a nursing home.

Thanks to the team of professionals at UI Health, I am now out of my wheelchair and using a cane.

My stroke helped me see myself clearly for the first time. I was shocked to realize how crazy my life had become. It gave me the opportunity to slow down and strengthen relationships through communication and spending quality time together. I never realized how much time the UI Health team invests in treating their patients. They hire people with exceptional skills as well as compassionate hearts. We are called champions, not survivors, because we motivate others to achieve something every day.

I believe the Lord used UI Health to save my life and help me live a renewed life. I've been allowed to use my deficits and challenges to help other stroke patients. I will always be grateful to UI Health.
